## Equation of a Grin lens:

	$$n(z,h)=n_0 + n_1h^2 + n_3z +n_4z^2$$
	
[[Flynn2024]]

### Properties of the Equation:

- $n_0$ is the refractive index at the surface
- $n_1$ will effect both height (change of magnitude) and shape (sign change makes it parabolic). Without $n_1$ the lens will be spherical, with it it will be elliptical unless made parabolic.

#### Rearranging for other formulae:

At the surface, $n(0,0)=n_0)$ so rearrange to get:

$$n1h^2=n_3z-n_4z^2$$
$$\Rightarrow h^2=\frac{n_3}{n_1}-\frac{n_4}{n_1}$$
where:
$$2R=\frac{n_3}{n_1}$$
with $R=radius\ of\ curvature$.

Conic Constant:

$$k=\frac{n_4}{n_1}-1$$
Controls how conic the ellipse/parabola is.
